# Event Reflection: "FCAJ Community Day - June 2026"

### Purpose of the Event

- Learn about the latest trends in AI-powered application development.
- Gain practical insights into cloud infrastructure operations and modernization.
- Explore how AI Agents (such as Amazon Q and DevOps Agents) can automate and optimize system operations.
- Connect with the Cloud and AI community in Vietnam.

### Event Highlights

#### Challenges of Traditional System Operations

- The impact of **technical debt** and the increasing complexity of large-scale systems.
- Limitations of manual operations, which require significant time and operational costs.
- Infrastructure incidents often take hours to investigate and resolve instead of minutes.

#### AI-Driven Operations & Cloud Architecture

- **Agentic Platform:** Integrating AI Agents for system monitoring, security assessments, and cost optimization (FinOps).
- **Multi-Agent Architecture:** Assigning specialized AI Agents to manage different components of a system, such as clusters, databases, and security services.
- **DevOps Agents:** Using AI to analyze incidents and automatically recommend mitigation plans based on the system architecture.

#### Amazon Q & Modernization

- **AI throughout the SDLC:** Using Amazon Q to support developers during coding, testing, and deployment.
- **Model Context Protocol (MCP):** A mechanism that connects AI with external data sources such as Jira, GitHub, Gmail, Outlook, and AWS resources, enabling AI to better understand project context.
- **VPC Connectivity & Security:** Protecting enterprise data by connecting AI Agents to internal environments through secure private channels such as AWS PrivateLink and Application Load Balancer (ALB).

### Key Learnings

#### A New Operations Mindset (AI-First Operations)

- **Context Awareness:** AI performs much better when provided with complete contextual information, including system topology, resource relationships, and operational logs.
- **Human-in-the-loop:** AI is not a replacement for engineers. Instead, it provides recommendations that help engineers make faster and more informed decisions.
- **Problem-Driven Development:** Rather than focusing solely on theory, prioritize solving real business challenges and improving operational workflows.

#### Practical Applications

- **Experiment with Amazon Q:** Register for and explore Amazon Q Developer and DevOps Agents to support software development and incident response in real projects.
- **Adopt an Agentic Mindset:** Research opportunities to integrate AI tools into existing team workflows, such as combining Slack or Jira with AI-powered assistants.
- **Improve Recruitment Screening:** Apply AI to analyze job descriptions and resumes, helping HR teams reduce manual workload and improve efficiency.

#### Event Experience

- **Learning from Experts:** Gained valuable insights from AWS professionals and startup engineers who shared their real-world experience in building products from concept to production.
- **Live Demonstrations:** Observed AI Agents detecting a DDoS attack and generating mitigation recommendations within minutes.
- **Community Networking:** Connected with Cloud and AI engineers, gaining a broader understanding of how organizations adopt emerging technologies.

#### Key Takeaways

- **Speed of Transformation:** AI can reduce incident investigation time from several days to just a few minutes.
- **Security First:** Always prioritize private and secure connectivity, such as VPCs and private endpoints, when integrating AI into enterprise systems.
- **Hands-on Mindset:** Start experimenting with small real-world problems instead of spending too much time planning.
